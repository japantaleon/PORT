Slandit.

Becoming a pro skater is the most challenging level a skateboarding can achieve. A skater must land a sponsorship to begin their journey to the pro level which is quite difficult to achieve. With 6.4 million skaters in the United States alone, competition Is everywhere, making it even harder to get notice. While other sports have clear paths on reaching the pro level, the path to sponsorship is so cult status that it’s almost unknown.

With this project, I aimed to create a clear path with the use of an App to allow skater to achieve their dream or at least land a sponsorship!

How it works now

I wanted to understand the current process and theres no better way than to hear it straight from the Pro’s! This video on how Sean Malto one of the most iconic skaters of his generation got sponsored, gave me great insight that I converted into a User Journey.

Inefficient and lacking

Understanding the process brought to my attention how little resources skaters have on differentiating themselves in order to get notice. With thousands of videos coming to a company, I realized the way the “Sponsor Me” tape is packaged needed to be changed. Other clear problems:

Too many videos/profiles landing on one Team Manager

Exposure is needed for companies and skaters alike.

Mailing video content not efficient

Skaters have very little leverage.

Sticky Note Ideas

I noted down some questions on post-it notes that I felt if answered, could help create a more efficient path for a Mobile solution!

A New Journey

I created a new User Journey with the Vision of this App in mind.

Making Slandit

I created the name by combing the words Skate and Land. The purpose of the App is to help skaters “land” a “Skate” sponsorship. Thus, by helping skaters “land it” we get Slandit! The logo actually resembles a skateboard bearing, just as those tiny bearings move a skater around, I hope the app can (hypothetically) move their careers along.

Gamification - Ranks

I wanted to make the experience fun by adding ranks. These are 4 ranks classified in the skateboarding community.
Shop: a skater that has local skate shop sponsors.
Flo: a skater that has several sponsors including mainstream companies.
Am/amateur: a skater that gets paid from their sponsors and you will see them on tour with the Pro’s.
Pro: a professional skater!
The icon’s design changes as they rank up. The higher it goes, the more it resembles the Slandit logo.

Building the Profile UI

I used my initial questions and flow charts to wireframe the UI.

Slandit Profile UI

How talented is he? See Videos
Are they cool? Get to know w/ Bio
How do I reach him? Email / Message
Can I offer to Sponsor him? Display Shop, Am, Flo, Pro
What can I give him? Horizontal Scroll View Sponsors
